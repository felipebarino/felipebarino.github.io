Felipe Oliveira Barino

Is a curious learner who believes that through the scientific method one could find a solution to any problem, or at least improve the present solution. He studied electrical engineering at the Federal University of Juiz de Fora (UFJF) between 2014 and 2019. He earned his Master's degree from UFJF degree in 2021. Nowadays he is a PhD student at the PPEE/UFJF, where he study novel techniques to process and calibrate optical fiber sensors. His current research interests are related to instrumentation, metrology, signal processing, and machine learning.

Experience

Has experience in sensor calibration and validation; machine learning development; and development of supervisory software for calibration.
Works at the instrumentation and telemetry laboratory (UFJF) since 2017, where he worked on several research and development programs.

Research

Fiber sensor manufacturing

Simulation, manufacturing, and validation of optical fiber devices used to measure a wide range of measurand. Development of new sensors and new embedment techniques for well-established sensors

Sensor signal processing

Information retrieval of optical devices, through the spectrum or filter-sampled optical power, to estimate characteristics useful for sensor calibration.

Applied sensing

Close the gap between the research lab and the industry. Solve open problems through optical fiber sensing.

Machine learning

Research and development of novel machine learning models to process sensor data. Implementing such models on collected data to predict trends, and perform data segmentation and classification.
